LETCHON PAKSIW

Lechon paksiw is a flavorful Filipino dish that transforms leftover roasted pork into a delectable stew, simmered in a sweet and tangy sauce.


Here's a list of ingredients for lechon paksiw:

  • Leftover lechon (roast pork):

    Tender and flavorful roast pork, typically with crispy skin, leftover from a previous meal.

  • Vinegar:

    Adds a tangy acidity to the dish, balancing the richness of the pork and enhancing its flavor.

  • Soy sauce:

    Provides a savory umami flavor and adds depth to the sauce

  • Garlic:

    Infuses the dish with aromatic flavor, complementing the richness of the pork.

  • Onions:

    Adds sweetness and depth of flavor to the sauce, while also providing texture.

  • Bay leaves:

    Imparts a subtle earthy aroma and flavor to the dish.

  • Peppercorns:

    Contribute a mild spiciness and aromatic warmth to the sauce.

  • Sugar:

    Balances the acidity of the vinegar and adds a touch of sweetness to the dish.

  • Liver spread or pineapple juice (optional):

    Liver spread adds richness and depth to the sauce, while pineapple juice can provide sweetness and a hint of tropical flavor, enhancing the overall complexity of the dish.



Here's a step-by-step procedure to make lechon paksiw:

  1. Prepare the Ingredients:
    • Gather your leftover lechon (roast pork), vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, onions, bay leaves, peppercorns, sugar, and any optional ingredients like liver spread or pineapple juice.

  2. Slice and Prepare the Lechon:
    • Cut the leftover lechon into bite-sized pieces, ensuring that you include both meat and crispy skin for maximum flavor and texture.

  3. Make the Sauce:
    • In a pot or large saucepan, combine 1 cup of vinegar, 1/2 cup of soy sauce, 4 cloves of minced garlic, 1 sliced onion, 2 bay leaves, 1 teaspoon of peppercorns, and 2 tablespoons of sugar.
    • If using, add 2 tablespoons of liver spread or 1/2 cup of pineapple juice to the sauce for added richness or sweetness.

  4. Simmer the Sauce:
    • Bring the sauce mixture to a gentle simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally to dissolve the sugar and allow the flavors to meld together.

  5. Add the Lechon:
    • Once the sauce is simmering, add the sliced lechon pieces into the pot, ensuring they are fully submerged in the sauce.

  6. Simmer and Stew:
    • Let the lechon simmer in the sauce for about 20-30 minutes, or until the meat becomes tender and absorbs the flavors of the sauce.
    • Stir occasionally to prevent sticking and ensure even cooking.

  7. Adjust the Seasoning:
    • Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning as needed, adding more soy sauce for saltiness, vinegar for acidity, or sugar for sweetness according to your preference.

  8. Serve:
    • Once the lechon is tender and the sauce has thickened slightly, remove the pot from the heat.
    • Transfer the lechon paksiw to a serving dish and garnish with additional sliced onions or chopped spring onions if desired.

Enjoy your delicious homemade Lechon Paksiw! Adjust the seasonings and ingredients according to your taste preferences for a personalized touch.
