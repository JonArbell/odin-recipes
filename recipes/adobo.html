ADOBO

Adobo is a popular Filipino dish known for its savory and tangy flavors.


Here are the ingredients typically used in adobo, along with a brief description:

  • Chicken or Pork:

    Adobo can be made with either chicken or pork, or sometimes a combination of both. The meat is typically cut into pieces and marinated in a mixture of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and spices.

  • Vinegar:

    Vinegar is a key ingredient in adobo, providing its distinctive tangy flavor. It also helps to tenderize the meat during the cooking process.

  • Soy Sauce:

    Soy sauce adds a salty and savory flavor to adobo. It also contributes to the rich, dark color of the dish.

  • Garlic:

    Garlic is used to flavor the adobo marinade, providing a robust and aromatic element to the dish.

  • Bay Leaves:

    Bay Leaves are often added to adobo for their earthy and slightly floral flavor. They infuse the dish with subtle herbal notes.

  • Whole Peppercorns:

    Whole Peppercorns are added to the adobo marinade to provide a hint of spiciness and depth of flavor.

  • Water or Broth:

    Water or Broth is used to dilute the marinade and create the cooking liquid for simmering the meat until tender.

  • Oil:

    Oil is used for sautéing the garlic and searing the meat before simmering it in the marinade, adding richness to the dish.


Here's a step-by-step procedure to make Filipino adobo:

  1. Prepare the Ingredients:
    • Gather the ingredients: chicken or pork, vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, whole peppercorns, water or broth, and oil.
    • Cut the meat into serving pieces if necessary, and peel and mince the garlic.

  2. Marinate the Meat:
    • In a bowl or container, combine the meat, minced garlic, soy sauce, vinegar, bay leaves, and whole peppercorns.
    • Make sure the meat is evenly coated with the marinade.
    • Let the meat marinate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.

  3. Cook the Adobo:
    • Heat oil in a large skillet or pot over medium heat.
    • Once the oil is hot, add the marinated meat along with the marinade.
    • Sauté the meat for a few minutes until it's browned on all sides.

  4. Simmer the Adobo:
    • Pour water or broth into the skillet or pot, enough to cover the meat.
    • Bring the liquid to a boil, then reduce the heat to low.
    • Cover and simmer the adobo for about 30 minutes to 1 hour, or until the meat is tender and fully cooked.

  5. Adjust the Seasoning:
    • Taste the adobo and adjust the seasoning if necessary.
    • You can add more soy sauce, vinegar, or salt according to your preference.

  6. Serve:
    • Once the meat is tender and the sauce has thickened slightly, remove the bay leaves and discard them.
    • Transfer the adobo to a serving dish and garnish with some chopped green onions or cilantro if desired.
    • Serve hot with steamed rice.

Enjoy your homemade Filipino Adobo, a delicious and comforting dish with a perfect balance of savory and tangy flavors!
